## CPA Handoff Checklist

- Ask vendors for line-item invoices, not one lump-sum build quote.
- Keep titles, VINs, serial numbers, inspection approvals, and placed-in-service dates.
- Separate truck/trailer purchase from buildout labor and equipment wherever possible.
- Separate refundable deposits from actual fees.
- Separate inventory and packaging from durable equipment.
- Track sales tax collected separately from sales revenue in the POS.
- Decide with the CPA whether to use Section 179, bonus depreciation, regular depreciation, or a mix.
- Ask whether S corporation taxation is worth evaluating after consistent profit is proven.
- Confirm how owner health insurance, retirement contributions, and home-office/admin expenses should be handled.
- Keep a mileage/travel log even if the truck itself uses actual-expense treatment.

## Practical Recommendation

Do not build projections around a single net number. Use three cases:

| Case | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Operating case | Shows whether events actually make money before tax strategy |
| Conservative tax case | Uses normal depreciation and avoids overpromising year-one deductions |
| Accelerated tax case | Shows cash benefit if Section 179/bonus depreciation is used |

The accelerated tax case may improve year-one cash flow, but it can reduce deductions available in later years. The decision should be made after the CPA sees the final build invoice, financing terms, expected profit, and household tax picture.
