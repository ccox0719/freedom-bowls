## Executive Overview

Freedom Bowls is a rice bowl food truck concept built around high-throughput service, batchable prep, sous-vide or retherm proteins, and flexible sauces/toppings. The current startup plan assumes a direct-to-truck launch with a used enclosed trailer or truck build, commercial cook line, refrigeration, sanitation, power, hood/fire suppression, smallwares, and POS setup.

The current startup cost range is **$47,000 to $75,000**, with a practical midpoint of **$61,000**. Based on the event plans already in the project, the payback range is approximately:

| Scenario | Annual gross | Annual operating net | Cash after 20% reserve | Payback at $47k | Payback at $61k | Payback at $75k |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Low Stress Retirement Style | $116,000 | $36,250 | $29,000 | 1.6 yrs | 2.1 yrs | 2.6 yrs |
| Balanced First Year | $150,500 | $43,625 | $34,900 | 1.3 yrs | 1.7 yrs | 2.1 yrs |
| Aggressive Growth Year | $241,000 | $71,975 | $57,580 | 0.8 yrs | 1.1 yrs | 1.3 yrs |

The recommended investor story is a **24-month target payback** on a midpoint raise of about **$61,000**, funded from operating cash flow after holding back a 20% reserve for repairs, working capital, taxes, and slow periods.

The smartest startup route remains a used enclosed trailer or used step van with a commercial-grade custom build. The key cost risk is hood/fire suppression plus vehicle buildout. A contingency reserve should be kept until health inspection and final equipment installation are complete.

### Service Model

The food truck should be built for event service rather than slow daily street vending. The operating target is fast bowl assembly for 300 to 500 guest capability at larger events.

| Operating area | Plan |
|---|---|
| Primary production flow | Rice, protein, toppings, sauce, packaging |
| Main bottleneck | Protein finishing on the flat top |
| Best-fit service | Corporate lunches, catering, breweries, festivals |
| Typical staffing | 2 people for standard events, 3 for festival days |
| Production strategy | Batch prep before events, fast finishing and assembly on site |

### Staffing

Most standard events should be planned around two-person service. Festival days should use three people because service hours are longer, demand is more concentrated, and restocking/line management matters more.

## Menu and Production

### Concept

Freedom Bowls sells rice bowls designed for speed, portability, and event volume. The operating model should prioritize:

- A small menu with repeatable base components.
- Batch-prepped rice, sauces, toppings, and proteins.
- Fast service for festivals, brewery nights, corporate lunches, and private catering.
- Catering and festival bookings as the highest-leverage revenue channels.

The concept is well suited to 300 to 500 guest events because rice bowls are easy to stage, portion, and customize without turning the truck into a full made-to-order kitchen.

### Menu Controls

| Control | Purpose |
|---|---|
| 3 to 5 core bowls | Keeps ordering simple and service fast |
| 2 to 3 proteins | Reduces flat-top bottlenecks |
| Rotating sauce or seasonal feature | Adds variety without operational sprawl |
| Recipe costing system | Keeps ingredient costs tied to actual menu margin |
| Event prep lists | Reduces waste and forgotten items |

Operational priorities:

- Prep sauces, toppings, and proteins in bulk using the existing recipe costing system.
- Use rice cookers, hot holding, and a flat top as the main production flow.
- Track actual food cost by recipe and menu item after each invoice cycle.
- Track event-level gross, labor, food cost, waste, and net after every event.

### Sales Channels

| Channel | Role in the plan |
|---|---|
| Corporate lunches | Predictable weekday revenue, good repeat potential |
| Private catering | Higher average net, better pre-planning, lower walk-up risk |
| Festivals | Highest gross per event, but higher weather and fee risk |
| Brewery nights | Reliable local visibility and weekday volume |
| Community pop-ups | Brand building, menu testing, social proof |

The first-year sales plan should move from public pop-ups toward prepaid or semi-guaranteed catering. Catering lowers demand risk and improves prep planning.

### Payback Strategy

Use operating net conservatively. The payback model holds back **20% of annual operating net** before calculating investor repayment capacity.

At the midpoint investment need of **$61,000**, the Balanced First Year plan produces roughly **$34,900** in annual cash available after reserve, or about **$2,908 per month**. A 24-month payback on $61,000 requires about **$2,542 per month**, so the Balanced plan clears the target at approximately **1.14x coverage**.

## Investor Structure

| Item | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Capital raise target | $61,000 midpoint, with authority to use up to $75,000 if buildout costs require it |
| Payback target | 24 months from launch |
| Reserve policy | Hold back 20% of operating net before investor distributions |
| Distribution method | Monthly distributions after reserve, capped until original capital is repaid |
| Investor upside | Optional fixed premium or profit share after capital repayment, negotiated separately |
| Reporting | Monthly event P&L summary, quarterly investor update |

Do not promise a fixed monthly payment unless the investor understands seasonality. A better structure is a monthly sweep from available cash with a 24-month target and transparent quarterly reporting.

## Risk Management

| Risk | Control |
|---|---|
| Buildout overruns | Use written vendor quotes, keep 10% contingency, avoid unnecessary equipment upgrades |
| Low early bookings | Pre-sell catering before launch, build a 90-day event calendar before opening |
| Food cost creep | Update ingredient costs monthly and keep menu price tied to actual cost per bowl |
| Labor inefficiency | Standardize prep lists and event pack lists; use two-person service for most events |
| Weather and seasonality | Build catering pipeline for indoor and private events |
| Equipment downtime | Keep repair reserve and prioritize simple, serviceable equipment |

## Launch Milestones

| Timeline | Milestone |
|---|---|
| Pre-launch | Finalize truck/trailer path, quotes, permits, and health department requirements |
| 30 days before launch | Book first 10 to 15 events, confirm supplier pricing, test prep workflow |
| First 90 days | Complete 20 to 25 events, measure actual net by event type, refine menu |
| Month 6 | Reach consistent 7 to 9 events per month or equivalent catering revenue |
| Month 12 | Hit at least the Balanced First Year run rate and validate payback coverage |
| Month 24 | Target investor capital fully repaid under the base case |

## Investor Pitch Summary

Freedom Bowls is a practical, high-throughput food truck concept with a startup capital need of **$47,000 to $75,000** and a base-case payback path of roughly **18 to 24 months** when cash reserves are maintained. The business does not need daily street service to work; it needs a disciplined calendar of corporate lunches, catering, breweries, festivals, and selective community events.

The investment case is strongest when presented as a capital-efficient local food business with visible equipment collateral, measurable event economics, and a clear path to repayment from booked event cash flow.
